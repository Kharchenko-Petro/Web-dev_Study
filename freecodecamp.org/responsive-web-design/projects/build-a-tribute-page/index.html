Jesus Christ

The One who saved the world

Historical facts about Jesus

  • About 4-6 years BC

    Born in Bethlehem, Judea

  • Less than two years later

    King Herod orders the killing of all children under the age of two. He wants to eliminate a potential contender for the throne. Jesus' parents were forced to flee Bethlehem to Egypt.

  • At the age of twelve

    Jesus, along with his family and all the people of Israel, visits Jerusalem for the feast and remains in the Temple. His parents find him in two days. In response to the question: "Why did you do so?" Jesus answered, "I must be in my Father's house." However, Joseph and Mary take him with them to Nazareth.

  • About 30 years

    Jesus receives baptism in water from John the Baptist and begins his public ministry. He proclaims the message of God to the people, saying, "The kingdom of God is at hand. Repent and believe this Good News."

  • Palm Sunday (six days before death)

    Jesus enters Jerusalem on a donkey before the Passover. A large crowd follows him. People see in the actions of Jesus the fulfillment of the prophecy of the coming of the King promised by God. They glorify Jesus. The Pharisees command the Lord to stop the people because they fear political resonance. Tensions between Jesus and religious authorities are growing.

  • Maundy Thursday (one day before death)

    Jesus celebrates Passover with his disciples. Judas Iscariot leaves dinner. After supper, Jesus goes with the disciples to the Garden of Gethsemane. Judas betrays Jesus. The slaves and the temple guard grab Jesus and lead him to the high priest's house. Jesus is being interrogated by the chief priests. He is sentenced to death.

  • Good Friday (day of death)

    Jesus is led to Pontius Pilate. The crowd demands to crucify him on the cross. Pilate betrays Jesus to the will of the people. Jesus and the two criminals are crucified on Calvary. Darkness sets in for three hours. Jesus is dying. His body is buried in a cave by Joseph of Arimathea.

  • Holy Saturday (second day after death)

    The body of Jesus in the tomb. The people of Israel rest from working according to God's command. Jesus' disciples mourn and hide.

  • Passover Sunday (third day after death)

    An angel appears near the tomb and rolls away a stone. The women come to the tomb but do not find the body. Two disciples run to the tomb and find only the veils in which the body was wrapped. Jesus meets and talks with two disciples on the way to Emmaus. Finally, the risen Jesus appears to the disciples in the upper room. He convinces the disciples that he has risen in the flesh.

"For God so loved the world that He gave His only begotten Son, that whoever believes in Him should not perish but have everlasting life." -- Holy Bible. Gospel of John 3:16
